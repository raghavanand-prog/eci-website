Recognization of Parties

The Election Commission of India (ECI) is responsible for the recognition of political parties in India. The recognition of parties is based on their performance in elections and their adherence to certain criteria set by the ECI. There are two types of recognition: National Party and State Party. A National Party is recognized if it secures at least 6% of the total valid votes in any four or more states in a general election to the Lok Sabha or Legislative Assembly, and wins at least four seats in the Lok Sabha from any state or states. A State Party is recognized if it secures at least 6% of the total valid votes in a state in a general election to the Legislative Assembly, and wins at least two seats in the Legislative Assembly of that state.

Recognized parties are entitled to certain privileges, such as reserved symbols, free airtime on state media during elections, and access to electoral rolls. The recognition of parties is crucial for the democratic process in India, as it helps to ensure a level playing field for all political parties and promotes a healthy competition in elections.
